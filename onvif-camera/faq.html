Recommendation for Initial Setup

We highly recommend that all users first visit the 'Settings' page in our app to configure key aspects of their streaming experience. This includes setting the video resolution, frames per second (fps), and audio quality to levels that best suit your needs and device capabilities. Resolution: Choose the appropriate resolution based on the balance between video clarity and your device’s processing power, as well as network bandwidth. A standard setting of 1280x720 is often sufficient, but feel free to adjust based on your specific requirements. Frames Per Second (fps): Adjust the fps to find the right balance between smooth video playback and resource usage. The app offers Low (15 fps), Medium (25 fps), and High (30 fps) settings. The Medium setting is recommended for most users. Audio Quality: Select from High, Medium, and Low audio quality settings. These settings adjust the sample rate and bitrate to balance sound clarity with data usage. For general purposes, the Medium setting is often adequate. By carefully adjusting these settings, you can optimize the app’s performance, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable streaming experience while conserving your device’s resources such as battery life, CPU usage, and network bandwidth.

Q: What resolution should I set for optimal performance?

A: The appropriate resolution setting in the app depends on a balance among video clarity, network bandwidth, and battery usage. Higher resolutions provide clearer video quality, but they consume more bandwidth and drain battery life more quickly. A resolution of 1280x720 is generally recommended as it offers a good balance for most users. If you wish to enhance clarity, you can try increasing the resolution, but keep an eye on the playback performance in your player. Should you encounter lag, stuttering, or pixelation, it could indicate that the resolution is too high for your network's bandwidth or too demanding on your device's battery. In such cases, reducing the resolution can lead to smoother playback, less bandwidth consumption, and slower battery drain. Finding the right balance between resolution, network usage, and battery life is key to ensuring a satisfying user experience without overtaxing your device’s resources.

Q: What frames per second (fps) setting should I choose?

A: FPS, or frames per second, refers to the rate at which consecutive images (frames) appear on a display. Our app provides three preset options for fps: Low, Medium, and High, corresponding to 15, 25, and 30 fps, respectively. A higher fps results in smoother video but also leads to greater consumption of network bandwidth, CPU resources, and battery power. We generally recommend selecting the Medium option, which is 25 fps, as it offers a good balance between video fluidity and resource usage. This setting provides smooth video playback while minimizing the strain on your network, device performance, and battery life. However, you can adjust the fps according to your specific needs and the capabilities of your device and network.

Q: How should I set the audio quality in the app?

A: The app provides three preset levels for audio quality: High, Medium, and Low. These levels correspond to different combinations of sample rates and bitrates. Specifically, the High setting is at a sample rate of 44100 KHz with a bitrate of 128 Kbps. The Medium setting also uses a sample rate of 44100 KHz, but with a lower bitrate of 93 Kbps. Finally, the Low setting offers a sample rate of 16000 Hz with a bitrate of 64 Kbps. For most users, we recommend selecting the Medium quality level. This setting provides a good balance between audio clarity and resource usage, such as network bandwidth. It is suitable for general purposes where clear audio is desired without excessive consumption of data. You can adjust the settings according to your specific needs and the limitations of your device or network.

Q: Why does this app require Camera, Microphone, and Network permissions? Can I grant only some of these permissions? allow Cemera or Microphone

A: No, it is not possible to grant only some of these permissions for the app to function properly. This app is designed to capture and transmit audio and video data, which necessitates the use of Camera and Microphone permissions. The Camera permission is essential for accessing the device's camera to capture video. The Microphone permission is required to record audio, an integral part of the video streaming and recording functionalities. Additionally, the Network permission is crucial for the app as it provides ONVIF services, which involve transmitting the captured audio and video data over a network. This service is fundamental for remote access and control of the video stream, making the network connectivity a core component of the app’s functionality. Without all three permissions - Camera, Microphone, and Network - the app cannot perform its intended tasks, such as streaming and capturing audio-video content, and providing ONVIF compliant services to users.

Q:Can this app run in the background?

A: Yes, the app is capable of running in the background, and we recommend doing so. Running in the background allows the app to reduce resource usage, particularly in terms of video rendering in the foreground. However, if you encounter any issues with the app while it's running in the background, you have the option to switch it to the foreground. To further decrease resource consumption, you can navigate to the 'Settings' or 'Help' pages within the app. This maneuver significantly lowers resource usage as these pages are less resource-intensive compared to the main streaming or capturing functionalities. Please note that when the app is in the background, avoid using other resource-heavy applications on your Android device. The Android system might close background apps to free up resources. Also, refrain from running apps that require Camera or Microphone access as they might interfere with the functionalities of our app. Ensuring these precautions can help maintain optimal performance of the app, providing uninterrupted ONVIF services whether in the foreground or background.

Q: Does this app collect user data?

A: No, our app does not collect any user data. We are committed to ensuring the privacy and security of our users. This extends to not storing any audio or video data captured through the Camera or Microphone on the user's device. All audio and video data processed by the app are used solely for providing ONVIF services and are not recorded, stored, or retained on the device's storage. This policy is in place to respect user privacy and to comply with data protection regulations. Users can utilize the app's features with the assurance that their personal data and the media content captured through their device's camera and microphone are not being collected or stored by our app.
